# Group Contract

## Team Purpose and Objectives

We are a group of students from the CSE 110 Spring 2026 class taught by Professor Thomas A. Powell and mentored by TA Helena Bender.

Our purpose as a team is to successfully complete the project as outlined by the course syllabus, gain meaningful experience working in a software engineering team, and pass the project and course.

We want to build a final project that we can be proud of, where everyone is able to contribute and feel ownership over the final product. A key goal is for members to have the opportunity to learn new skills and explore different areas of software engineering and development.

## Values and Standards

### Communication Standards

All team communication will occur through the team Slack. We value the principles of clear and respectful communication, being open to constructive feedback, and being accountable and supportive to other teammates. In order to facilitate this, members will follow the following guidelines:

* Communicating ahead of time if additional time or support is needed.
* Offering help or feedback in a respectful and constructive manner.
* Completing tasks in a timely manner, especially if others' tasks depend on prior completion.
* Respecting others' time, work, and effort.

In addition, we want to facilitate a positive, collaborative working environment, and therefore encourage frequent, casual, and open communication through team Slack channels.

### Coding Standards

To maintain a high quality of code and a collaborative environment, team members will utilize good coding and documentation practices, including but not limited to:

* Consistent spacing and indentation (standard 4 space indentation).
* Clean and logical code styling.
* Method headers and inline comments.
* Descriptive commit messages and pull requests.
* Appropriate error testing.
* Clean and organized codebase and branching practices.

## Issue Prevention and Support

As a team, we would like to anticipate and prevent issues before they happen. As outlined in the Communication Standards, team members are encouraged to reach out early to request support, extra time, or voice concerns to the team. In the event where support is needed, team members will take action to redistribute work as appropriate.

## Accountability and Conflict Resolution

To maintain accountability, the team will focus on clear and open communication. For leadership positions, we encourage transparency in decision making and early communication with team members. Among the team, we encourage open review of others' work and quality of output.

In the event of a conflict (in particular, where one team member is not doing their fair share/ghosts), we will adhere to the following procedure:

1. Attempt to resolve internally through:
   1. Direct communication with the teammate.
   2. Redistribution of roles and responsibilities.
   3. Other methods of resolution within the team.
2. Escalation (only if necessary):
   1. Notifying the Mentor TA of lack of participation.
   2. Further escalation as necessary.

In general, we aim to resolve conflicts internally, and understand that emergencies and extraneous events occur. We encourage early communication to prevent unexpected conflicts and issues.
