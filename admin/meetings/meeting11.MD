## New Business
The team met over Zoom for a sprint review stand-up to reflect on the recent prototype work and discuss how the team’s planning process has been going. Each team member discussed what they've done and any blockers they had.

Nathan said that splitting into groups to test different prototypes was not a bad idea because it allowed the team to see different perspectives and compare different prototype approaches.

Ori agreed that splitting the team into smaller groups was helpful. He noted that it was easier to communicate ideas, decisions, and ADRs in smaller groups. He also felt that the whole team now has a better understanding of where the project is at. Ori mentioned that meeting in person is still better for discussion because when working with Claude or other AI tools, it can feel like every decision is correct, but talking with teammates helps reveal which choices should be questioned more carefully.

Humza agreed that smaller groups were better for communication. He also said that having more structure before jumping into actual coding is effective and helps the team avoid confusion later.

The team discussed that group size depends on the situation. Smaller groups may be better for focused prototype work, while larger group meetings may still be useful for major decisions and full-team alignment.

Angel said she will try to track stand-ups in GitHub so the team has a clearer record of progress, blockers, and updates.

The team also discussed product scope and customer needs. While the product can always be improved, the team noted that the scope should depend on the intended customer. The team questioned whether an over-the-top issue tracking system is necessary for intermediate or beginner user.

The team then discussed how issues should be prioritized and tracked. One concern was that if two users are working separately, they may accidentally create the same issue ID if it is their first issue. Nathan suggested possibly tracking time as part of the issue system. Ori suggested assigning issue IDs using a more complex formula that would make duplicate IDs extremely unlikely.

## Sprint Review Notes
- Splitting into prototype groups helped the team compare different ideas.
- Smaller groups made communication easier.
- Smaller groups helped with discussing ADRs and design decisions.
- In-person discussion is more effective than only working through AI tools.
- Team members should question AI-assisted decisions instead of assuming they are correct.
- More structure before coding can help the team work more effectively.
- Group size should depend on the type of work being done.
